# OPERATING / OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE
- **Tribal Energy Network (TEN)** owns, operates, and maintains EVSE + BESS through qualified subcontractors (Colusa Indian Energy, Allumia, 7 Generations, InCharge Energy).
- TOE receives a contractual share of **net operational benefits** (charging margin + battery savings/arbitrage). Define waterfall plainly: revenue → energy/network/maintenance/compliance costs → TEN operating margin → TOE distributions.
- **TOE incurs no operating expense.** TEN covers compliance, DD, ops, and maintenance prior to TOE remittance.
- **Sovereignty pathway:** TOE retains the right to take ownership after a **capacity certification** facilitated by TEN.
- **Contingency:** if NEVI is not awarded, proceed with the battery + energy-efficiency/electrification scope using remaining capital tools — "resilience-first" fallback.

Build a prominent, animated **"Tribes Signed On" thermometer** module. It must appear on the homepage above the fold (or immediately below the hero) and on a dedicated **/coalition** page. The component creates a bandwagon effect by visualizing collective progress.

## Visual & UX requirements
- Horizontal thermometer (or vertical on mobile) with a fill that animates from 0 → current count on first viewport entry (IntersectionObserver, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored).
- The bar's full width represents the **current goal** (`currentGoal`, starts at 5), not 20. When `tribesSignedOn` reaches `currentGoal`, the operator advances `currentGoal` to the next milestone in `data/coalition.json` and the bar resets/scales accordingly.
- Above the bar: `{N} of {currentGoal} Tribes signed on` and a `Last updated {date}` line. At launch this reads `0 of 5 Tribes signed on`.
- Below the bar: a one-line pointer — "Next milestone at {currentGoal} Tribes — {label}".
- Beneath the active bar, render a separate **"Stretch goals"** strip listing the remaining milestones (10 / 15 / 20) in a muted state, each with its label and unlock copy. These are visible context, not part of the active scale.
- Once a milestone is hit, mark its tick with a check glyph and accent color; promote the next milestone from "stretch" to "active".
- Use the Amerind Nation paper/ink palette and typography (Libre Caslon Text + IBM Plex Sans/Mono). Match the existing site — do not introduce new fonts or accent hues.
- Provide a "Bring your Tribe" CTA next to the thermometer linking to /ca-nevi-6 site-eligibility form.

# 20 FAQS (verbatim)
# =========================================================
1) **What is CA NEVI 6?** California's next funding round under the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure program to deploy public DC fast charging along designated corridors. We support Tribes in site readiness, application, award compliance, and delivery.
2) **What's in the $5M per-site package?** A $1.5M EV fast charging build (1.44 MW, 8×180 kW with CCS + NACS) plus a $3.5M battery system (1 MW / 6 MWh), including development, delivery coordination, and subcontracted O&M support.
3) **How much cash does the Tribe put in?** $150,000 total via a wholly-owned TOE in CD-secured escrow, released in milestone tranches. No additional Tribal equity required in the base model.
4) **Why does the Tribe contribute cash at all?** Aligns incentives, supports pre-development momentum, and helps unlock the broader capital stack while keeping Tribal exposure capped and predictable.
5) **Is the SSBCI/CDFI facility free money?** No — it is repayable financing used to bridge timing gaps and reduce friction. Repaid from project cash flows and/or reimbursements.
6) **Is NMTC guaranteed?** No. NMTC is allocation-based and requires CDE allocation. We model a target net benefit and pursue it at the portfolio level.
7) **How does 48E elective pay work for Tribes?** Eligible Tribal entities can receive the credit value as a refundable payment, subject to wage/apprenticeship rules, domestic content, and compliance.
8) **What is SGIP?** California's Self-Generation Incentive Program — incentive for energy storage. Availability depends on program step/rates and eligibility.
9) **What if NEVI isn't awarded?** Proceed with the battery + targeted energy improvements. EV charging can be pursued through other grant programs or later rounds.
10) **Who owns the equipment?** TEN initially owns and operates. TOE receives a contractual share of net operational benefits, with a path to Tribal ownership via capacity certification.
11) **What is capacity certification?** Verification the Tribe/TOE can assume ownership responsibly (operations, maintenance, compliance). TEN facilitates the process.
12) **Do we need to hire staff?** No. TOE incurs no operating expense in the base structure; TEN manages O&M via subcontractors.
13) **How is charging margin calculated?** Charging revenue minus energy costs, network/CPO fees, maintenance, and other operating expenses. Transparent waterfall and reporting in the agreement.
14) **How does the battery save/make money?** Reducing peak demand costs, shifting energy usage to lower-cost periods, supporting reliability, and capturing permissible arbitrage/savings depending on tariffs and operating rules.
15) **Is this a microgrid?** Microgrid-ready. True microgrid operation depends on site controls, interconnection permissions, and additional generation/controls.
16) **Does this include LCFS revenue?** Presented before LCFS — treated as incremental upside if captured contractually.
17) **What are the biggest risks?** Grant award uncertainty, reimbursement/credit timing, SGIP step availability, NMTC allocation availability, operational utilization. Mitigated by portfolio structuring, compliance-first procurement, conservative underwriting.
18) **How long does deployment take?** Day 1 kickoff → Day 90+ submission → Day 180+ award → Day 270+ delivery → Day 450+ substantial completion. Actual timing depends on interconnection and permitting.
19) **Can this scale?** Yes — designed for 2–20 Tribes with standardized package, portfolio procurement, and repeatable compliance.
20) **Is this legal/tax advice?** No. Planning information only. Final structures and eligibility confirmed with qualified counsel and program administrators.
